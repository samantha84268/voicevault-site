FAQ
Questions & answers

How does VoiceVault access my voicemails?

VoiceVault reads your iPhone backup (the same backup Finder creates when you plug in your phone). Your voicemails are stored in that backup file. We extract and organize them — no jailbreak, no hacking, no special access needed.

Do I need to create a backup first?

If you've ever plugged your iPhone into your Mac, you probably already have one. VoiceVault auto-detects existing backups. If you don't have one, just plug in your iPhone and back up via Finder — takes a few minutes.

Is my data private?

100%. Everything runs on your Mac. No servers, no accounts, no data uploads. Your voicemails never leave your computer. Transcription runs on-device using Apple's Speech framework.

Does it work with encrypted backups?

Currently VoiceVault works with unencrypted backups. If your backup is encrypted, you'll need to create an unencrypted one via Finder (uncheck "Encrypt local backup"). We're working on encrypted backup support.

What format are the voicemails saved in?

Standard audio files (.m4a) plus text transcripts (.txt). You can export them anytime. No proprietary formats, no lock-in.

Is there a subscription?

No. One purchase, yours forever. Free updates included. We believe the voices of your loved ones shouldn't come with a monthly bill.

What about new voicemails after the initial import?

Two options: plug into your Mac again and VoiceVault will pick up any new ones, or use the free iPhone companion app to save individual voicemails as they come in.
